## Who to contact, in order

**1. Ask in the HAAG `validated-pace-users` Slack channel first.**
This is a dedicated, student-led peer-support space built specifically so PACE/HPC questions don't get lost in general channels or repeated over and over. It's the fastest path for common issues, since it's staffed by other researchers who've likely hit the same problem. Joining requires basic PACE preparedness — it's a validated channel, not open to everyone by default, which keeps discussion focused and relevant.

**2. Run 303's validator and check 304's system status page** before escalating further — both take under a minute and rule out the two most common causes of "my job just isn't working": a script problem the validator would catch, or a PACE-side issue that's out of your control.

**3. Email PACE directly** at **pace-support@oit.gatech.edu** if the issue is specific to your account, allocation, or something Slack peers can't resolve. PACE's support team routes inquiries to the right staff member.

**4. Attend a free weekly virtual consultation session** for anything that needs a real conversation rather than an email thread — times and connection details are on the [Consultation Sessions page](https://gatech.service-now.com/home?id=kb_article_view&sysparm_article=KB0042280).

## Get a faster answer

When you do reach out — Slack, email, or consultation — include:

- The exact error message and the exact commands that led to it
- Where the job script or program lives
- What you've already tried
- Any other symptoms that might be related

This is the difference between a same-day answer and a week of back-and-forth.

## Policies worth knowing

PACE's [acceptable use and account policies](https://gatech.service-now.com/home?id=kb_article_view&sysparm_article=KB0042198) cover allocation rules and account standards. Worth a skim once, not something to memorize.

## A note on contact info

PACE's own current support page lists **pace-support@oit.gatech.edu** as the primary contact. Some older HAAG materials reference **hpchelp@gatech.edu** instead — worth confirming with PACE which is current before this goes out broadly, so we don't point researchers at a possibly-retired address.
